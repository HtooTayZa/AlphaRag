FACT  HUB  MYANMAR
 
Editorial  Board  —  Workflow  Policy  Update  
 
1.  Editorial  Override  
For  articles  classified  as  News,  Seasonal  Articles,  Life-Saving  Tips  During  a  Natural  
Disaster,
 
or
 
Follow-Up
 
Articles,
 
content
 
writers
 
are
 
permitted
 
to
 
temporarily
 
bypass
 
the
 
standard
 
editorial
 
process
 
and
 
proceed
 
directly
 
to
 
the
 
publishing
 
stage.
 
 
All
 
coordination
 
between
 
each
 
stage
 
or
 
process
 
must
 
be
 
handled
 
by
 
assigned
 
members.
  
1.1  Eligibility  ●  The  Content  Head  determines  whether  a  piece  of  content  qualifies  for  the  
override
 
during
 
the
 
content
 
proposal
 
stage,
 
subject
 
to
 
CEO
 
approval.
  ●  Only  after  the  Content  Head  or  the  Founder  grants  explicit  permission  may  
the
 
content
 
advance
 
to
 
the
 
publishing
 
stage.
 
The
 
CEO
 
holds
 
final
 
authority
 
over
 
all
 
decisions.
  ●  A  "Follow-Up  Article"  is  defined  as  an  article  published  within  12  days  of  a  
prior
 
related
 
article.
 
It
 
must
 
be
 
either
 
fact-checking
 
or
 
informative
 
in
 
nature.
  ●  Content  must  fall  strictly  within  one  or  more  of  the  eligible  categories  listed  
above.
  ●  Information  within  the  content  must  be  directly  relevant  to  current  
circumstances
 
at
 
the
 
time
 
of
 
publication.
  ●  The  Editorial  Override  may  be  invoked  a  maximum  of  5  times  per  month  
across
 
all
 
content.
